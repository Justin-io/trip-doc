PROJECT NEXUS

The Ultimate Cyber-Arcade Experience

Prepared For: TechFest 2026 Organizing Committee
Theme: "Gamifying the Future: From AI to Cybersecurity"

1. Executive Summary

"Project Nexus" is not just a technical exhibition; it is a fully interactive Cyber-Arcade. Unlike traditional fests where students passively look at screens, this event turns every attendee into a "Player."

Using a centralized Coin System, students will engage with 27 distinct projects across Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity, and IoT. By participating in games and challenges, they earn digital currency ("FestCoins") to redeem for rewards, ensuring maximum engagement and footfall.

2. The Core Mechanic: "FestCoin"

To drive participation, the entire event is gamified using a custom-built loop:

  1. The UID: Upon entry, every student receives a digital/physical badge with a unique QR Code.
  2. The Grind: Students visit project stalls and PLAY to EARN.
    • Win a match in Deadshot.io: +50 Coins
    • Beat the AI in Flappy Bird: +40 Coins
    • Pass the Password Strength Test: +25 Coins
  3. The Scan: Volunteers scan QR codes after wins, instantly updating the central database.
  4. The Leaderboard: Live projector display of the "Top 10 Players".
  5. The Redemption: Spend coins at the IoT Smart Dispenser or Prize Counter.

3. Event Zones & Projects

The 27 projects are organized into four distinct "Experience Zones."

ZONE A: THE ARENA (Competitive Gaming)

  • Deadshot.io Tournament: A browser-based FPS tournament. Students can choose to play 1v1 against other students OR challenge the "Sentinel AI" (a Computer Vision-powered bot) for double points.
  • Nose-Nav Flappy Bird: Hands-free gaming using facial landmark detection. Players control the bird by moving their nose up and down.
  • Invisible Cloak (Optical Camouflage): An immersive "Harry Potter" style experience using OpenCV background subtraction. Perfect for photo ops.

ZONE B: THE CYBER-LAB (Security & Awareness)

  • The Phishing Tank: A "Spot the Fake" challenge. Can students identify the phishing email or rogue WiFi?
  • Password Strength Meter: "The Uncrackable Challenge." AI calculates how many centuries it would take to crack the user's password.
  • Malware Defense Simulation (RAT): A controlled demo of a Remote Access Tool to show vulnerability.
  • OSINT Challenge: A race to find public information about a volunteer using only their email ID.

ZONE C: THE AI STUDIO (Generative Art)

  • AI Artist & Meme Maker: Attendees enter a prompt, and the AI generates a custom sticker/meme.
  • Sonic Identity: The system composes a unique melody based on the student's name/hometown.
  • The "Roast" Bot: An adversarial chatbot designed to have funny, witty arguments.
  • Deep-Scan: AI analyzes micro-expressions to generate a "RPG Character Sheet" (Charisma, Stress, etc.).

ZONE D: THE DEV SPRINTS (Coding & IoT)

  • 15-Minute Code Sprint: Rapid-fire coding competition.
  • Instant Website Challenge: Who can deploy a landing page fastest using AI tools?
  • IoT Smart Hydration: A functional hardware project. Scan QR (spend 10 Coins) to dispense a drink.

4. The "Knowledge Tracks"

Short 15-minute "Flash Talks" on the main stage:

  • Cybersecurity in 2026: Emerging threats and defenses.
  • Data Science Futures: Trends in big data.
  • IoT Dashboards: How to build the "Smart Hydration" system.

5. Technical Requirements

  • Hardware: 5-6 Laptops (Game Stations), 1 Projector (Leaderboard), IoT Sensors (Water Pump, Arduino).
  • Network: High-speed WiFi (Crucial for Deadshot.io and Cloud AI APIs).
  • Space: A darkened area for the "Invisible Cloak" and Projector visibility.
