SESSION CURRICULUM: DATA SCIENCE & AI 2026
Duration: 45 Minutes (Lecture & Demo) | Level: Intermediate
1. SESSION OBJECTIVE
To provide a deep dive into the "Data to Decision" pipeline and demystify the magic behind Generative AI. Students will understand the mathematical foundations of Neural Networks and the practical applications of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Diffusion Models.
2. AGENDA BREAKDOWN
Time Topic & Key Talking Points
0:00 - 10:00 The Data Science Lifecycle (Real-World Example)
- Scenario: "How Netflix Recommends Movies."
- Collection: Logs, ratings, watch time.
- Cleaning/EDA: Handling missing values, outliers.
- Modeling: Collaborative Filtering vs Content-Based Filtering.
- Deployment: A/B Testing and production scale.
10:00 - 20:00 Neural Networks Demystified (Visual/Interactive)
- The Neuron: Inputs, Weights, Bias, Activation Function (ReLU/Sigmoid).
- Architecture: Input Layer -> Hidden Layers (black box) -> Output Layer.
- Training: Loss Functions (MSE) and Backpropagation (Gradient Descent) explained simply as "learning from mistakes."
- Demo: Reference "Playground.tensorflow.org".
20:00 - 35:00 The Generative AI Revolution
- Discriminative vs Generative: "Is this a cat?" vs "Draw a cat."
- LLMs (GPT): The Transformer architecture (Attention Mechanism) and predicting the next token.
- Image Gen: Diffusion Models (Adding noise -> Removing noise) explained via the Zone C "AI Artist" project.
- RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation): Giving AI a "textbook" to reduce hallucinations.
35:00 - 40:00 Ethics, Bias & The Future
- Bias: Algorithmic discrimination (e.g., Hiring AI showing gender bias).
- Deepfakes: The erosion of truth.
- Job Market: AI as a Co-pilot, not a replacement.
40:00 - 45:00 Roadmap & Career Q&A
- Roles: Data Analyst vs Data Engineer vs ML Ops vs Research Scientist.
- Stack: Python, SQL, Pandas, PyTorch/TensorFlow.
- Q&A Session.
3. KEY CONCEPTS TO COVER
  • Supervised vs Unsupervised: Labeled data (Spam detection) vs Pattern finding (Customer segmentation).
  • Overfitting/Underfitting: The Goldilocks problem of modeling.
  • Feature Engineering: The art of creating meaningful inputs.
  • Prompt Engineering: Techniques (Zero-shot, Few-shot, Chain-of-Thought).
4. STUDENT RESOURCES
  • Libraries: Scikit-Learn (ML), Pandas (Data), HuggingFace (Transformers).
  • Datasets: Kaggle, UC Irvine Repository, Google Dataset Search.
  • Practice: Google Colab (Free GPUs), Fast.ai courses.
