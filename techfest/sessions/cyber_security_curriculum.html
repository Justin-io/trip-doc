SESSION CURRICULUM: CYBERSECURITY 2026
Duration: 45 Minutes (Workshop) | Level: Beginner to Intermediate
1. SESSION OBJECTIVE
To provide a comprehensive overview of modern cyber threats and defensive strategies. This session moves beyond basic awareness into technical demonstrations of how attacks happen (OSINT, Phishing, Injection) and how to build a robust "Personal Defense System."
2. AGENDA BREAKDOWN
Time Topic & Key Talking Points
0:00 - 10:00 The Threat Landscape & "Why You?"
- Global Stats: Ransomware costs, attack frequency (every 39 seconds).
- Case Studies: Breakdown of a recent major breach (e.g., 23andMe or a local university).
- The Myth: "I have nothing to hide." (Explain data aggregation and identity theft).
10:00 - 20:00 Social Engineering & OSINT (Live Demo)
- Phishing vs Spear Phishing: The psychology of clicking.
- OSINT Demo: Pick a volunteer or public figure and show how much data is visible (Maltego/Google Dorks).
- The "Evil Twin": Technical explanation of the WiFi vulnerability demoed in Zone B.
20:00 - 30:00 AI in CyberSec: The New Arms Race
- Offense: Using LLMs (WormGPT) to write polymorphic malware and perfect phishing emails.
- Defense: AI-driven anomaly detection (SIEM systems).
- Deepfakes: Real-time voice/video cloning attacks and verification challenges.
30:00 - 40:00 Practical Defense Workshop
- Hands-on: Setting up a Password Manager (Bitwarden/1Password).
- 2FA/MFA: SMS vs Authenticator Apps vs Hardware Keys (YubiKey).
- Browser Hygiene: Ad-blockers, clearing cookies, checking SSL certificates.
40:00 - 45:00 Career Roadmap & Q&A
- Paths: Red Team (Offense) vs Blue Team (Defense) vs Bug Bounty Hunting.
- Certs: CompTIA Security+ -> CEH -> OSCP.
- Resource: "TryHackMe" & "HackTheBox" guidance.
3. KEY CONCEPTS TO COVER
  • CIA Triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability (The holy trinity).
  • Zero Trust Architecture: "Never trust, always verify" - even inside the network.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM): How data is intercepted on public WiFi.
  • Encryption: Symmetric vs Asymmetric (Public/Private Key) basics.
4. STUDENT RESOURCES
  • Tools to Learn: Kali Linux (OS), Wireshark (Network Analysis), Burp Suite (Web Pentesting).
  • Websites: HaveIBeenPwned.com, OWASP Top 10.
  • Books: "The Art of Invisibility" by Kevin Mitnick.
