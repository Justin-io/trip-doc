SESSION CURRICULUM: CYBERSECURITY 2026
Duration: 15 Minutes (Flash Talk) | Level: Beginner to Intermediate
1. SESSION OBJECTIVE
To demystify modern cyber threats and defensive strategies for students. Moving beyond "don't share password" to understanding attack vectors like Social Engineering, IoT vulnerabilities, and AI-driven phishing.
2. AGENDA BREAKDOWN
Time Topic & Key Talking Points
0:00 - 3:00 The Threat Landscape (Stats & Shock)
- Global cost of cybercrime.
- Real-world breach examples (e.g., Recent College/Company hacks).
- Hook: "Your password is probably already on the Dark Web."
3:00 - 7:00 The Human Firewall (Social Engineering)
- Phishing vs Vishing vs Smishing.
- Demo: Show a cloned "Instagram Login Page" (from the Cyber-Gym zone).
- How OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) is used to track you.
7:00 - 11:00 AI in CyberSec (The Double-Edged Sword)
- Attackers: Using ChatGPT to write malware/phishing emails.
- Defenders: using AI to detect anomalies.
- Deepfakes: The new frontier of identity theft.
11:00 - 15:00 Practical Defense (Takeaways)
- Why 2FA is non-negotiable.
- Password Managers.
- Q&A.
3. KEY CONCEPTS TO COVER
  • CIA Triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability.
  • Zero Trust Architecture: "Never trust, always verify."
  • The "Evil Twin" Attack: Explain the WiFi vulnerability being demoed in Zone B.
4. STUDENT RESOURCES
  • Tool to learn: Kali Linux, Burp Suite, Wireshark.
  • Certification Path: CompTIA Security+ -> CEH -> OSCP.
  • Site: HaveIBeenPwned.com
